Checkpoint 01 · Breaking trail

Fragmented Data

Every run starts here. Agents are only as good as the ground under them — and at most companies, that ground is scattered across systems that were never meant to pull together.

Q1My data is fragmented across dozens of systems that don't talk to each other. Where do agents even start?
Q2How do we unify our data and make it agent-ready without a giant migration or overhauling everything?
Q3What does "agent-ready" actually mean — what's the bar our data has to clear?
Q4Do we need a new warehouse, lakehouse, or vector database before any of this works?
Q5What do we do about data that's stale, duplicated, or contradicts itself from one system to the next?
Q6Which data problems must be fixed before agents show up — and which can agents help fix themselves?
Q7How do the spreadsheets, PDFs, and tribal knowledge that run half the business make it onto the sled?

Checkpoint 02 · One picture of the business

The Company Brain

Unified data is table stakes. The compounding advantage is a living, governed picture of your business — context every agent can draw from, so each one runs smarter than the last.

Q1How do we develop a central company brain that captures context and makes every agent more effective?
Q2What is a company brain, concretely — and how is it different from our warehouse, our wiki, or RAG?
Q3How do we capture the context that lives in people's heads before it walks out the door?
Q4How do agents learn our vocabulary, our policies, our precedents — the way we do things here?
Q5Who owns and curates shared context so it stays current instead of rotting like the intranet did?
Q6How do we stop every team from quietly rebuilding the same context in their own silo?
Q7Can one brain serve finance, marketing, and ops at once — without turning into mush?
Stuck at this checkpoint? Write us →
Checkpoint 03 · The rigging

The Harness

A model is a strong dog. A harness is what turns strong dogs into a team you can steer, meter, and trust with a load. This checkpoint is where pilots become production.

Q1What does an agent harness actually do — and why can't we just call a model API and be done?
Q2How do I manage and control token spend before it becomes a line item nobody can explain?
Q3How do we monitor agents in production — what's the equivalent of APM for an agentic workforce?
Q4What happens when an agent fails halfway through a workflow that matters?
Q5How do we evaluate agent quality before they ship — and keep evaluating after?
Q6Should we build the harness ourselves, or deploy one that exists and make it ours?
Q7How do we swap in better models as they arrive without breaking everything hitched downstream?
Stuck at this checkpoint? Write us →
Checkpoint 04 · Who steers, who pulls

The Operating Model

The technology is the easy half. The harder half is how humans and agents actually share the work — handoffs, accountability, and the day-to-day rhythm of a mixed team.

Q1What does an operating model look like when agents and humans are interacting in the same workflows?
Q2Which decisions must keep a human in the loop — and which are safe to hand to the team?
Q3Who manages an agent? And who's accountable when it gets something wrong?
Q4How should handoffs between people and agents actually work, hour to hour?
Q5How do roles change when the doing moves to agents and the people move to steering?
Q6How do we measure agent work — the same scorecard as people, or something new?
Stuck at this checkpoint? Write us →
Checkpoint 05 · Every seat on the sled

Your People

An agentic workforce isn't something IT does to a company — it's something your people learn to drive. That includes the person reading this page.

Q1How do we give employees the agency to build and deploy their own agents?
Q2Where does self-serve agent building make sense — and where should it stay centralized?
Q3How do we keep a hundred home-grown agents from becoming next year's shadow IT?
Q4What guardrails let people experiment freely without putting the business at risk?
Q5How do we find and grow the people who'll become our best agent builders?
Q6Should I be using AI myself in my day-to-day work — or is that someone else's job?
Q7How do we train people to steer well when they've spent whole careers being valued for pulling?
Stuck at this checkpoint? Write us →
Checkpoint 06 · Reading the ice

Safe Ice

A good musher knows which ice holds and which doesn't. Same discipline here: what agents may see, what they may touch, and how you prove it afterward.

Q1What data is safe to expose to agents — and what should never be?
Q2What tools should we be using to de-identify or mask our company's data before agents touch it?
Q3How do we scope what each agent can see and do — per team, per task, per customer?
Q4What audit trail do we need of what agents saw, decided, and did?
Q5How do we keep an agent from leaking one team's data to another — or into a vendor's model?
Q6What do our security team, our regulators, and our board need to see before we scale?
Q7When a model provider changes terms or models, what happens to the data we've already exposed?
Stuck at this checkpoint? Write us →
Checkpoint 07 · Cross-country

Beyond Your Four Walls

The biggest workflows don't end at your org chart — they run through suppliers, partners, agencies, and customers. Agents change what's possible between companies, not just within one.

Q1What are the best ways to use agents to collaborate with organizations outside our four walls?
Q2Can our agents work with our partners' and suppliers' agents — safely?
Q3How do we share just enough data with a partner's agents without giving away the farm?
Q4What protocols and agreements govern agent-to-agent work across company lines?
Q5Can agents run the connective tissue — chasing POs, reconciling invoices, syncing catalogs — between us and our partners?
Q6What does a clean room look like when both sides bring agents to it?
